# Case Study: SaaS Pricing Page Trial CTA

## Context

- **Product**: Project management SaaS (Linear-style)
- **Page/Screen**: Pricing page — free trial signup
- **Target Audience**: Engineering managers evaluating tools for their team
- **Agent(s) Used**: CTA Optimization Specialist

## Before (Original Content)

> Button: "Submit"
> No supporting text above or below the button.

## After (Agency Output)

> **CTA Variations:**
> 1. **"Start my free trial"** [RECOMMENDED] — *Ownership psychology*: "my" creates a sense of personal investment before they even begin
> 2. **"Try free for 14 days"** — *Specificity*: Concrete timeframe makes the commitment feel bounded and safe
> 3. **"Start building — it's free"** — *Outcome visualization*: Skips the "trial" framing and jumps to the value
> 4. **"Join 5,000+ engineering teams"** — *Social proof*: Validates the decision by showing peer adoption
> 5. **"Get started — no credit card"** — *Friction removal*: Directly addresses the biggest hesitation
>
> **Pre-CTA headline:** "Ready to ship faster?"
> **Post-CTA reassurance:** "Free for 14 days. No credit card required. Cancel anytime."
>
> **A/B test plan:** Test #1 vs #5. Hypothesis: Engineering managers are risk-averse with new tools — removing credit card friction (#5) will outperform ownership framing (#1) by 10-15% for this audience.

## What Changed & Why

| Change | Before | After | Principle |
|--------|--------|-------|-----------|
| Generic "Submit" | "Submit" | "Start my free trial" | Action verbs that name the outcome |
| No supporting context | Nothing around the CTA | Headline + reassurance text | CTAs don't exist in isolation |
| No risk reduction | Nothing addressing hesitation | "No credit card required. Cancel anytime." | Address objections at the point of decision |
| No social proof | Nothing | "5,000+ engineering teams" (variation) | Validate the decision with peer behavior |
| No testing plan | Single static CTA | 2 variations with hypothesis | CTAs should always be tested |

## Measurable Difference

| Metric | Before | After |
|--------|--------|-------|
| CTA word count | 1 ("Submit") | 4 ("Start my free trial") |
| Psychological principles used | 0 | 5 (ownership, specificity, visualization, social proof, friction removal) |
| Supporting copy | 0 words | 16 words (headline + reassurance) |
| Testable variations | 1 | 5 |
| Objections addressed | 0 | 3 (cost, commitment, credit card) |
