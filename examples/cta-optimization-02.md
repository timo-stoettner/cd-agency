# Case Study: E-Commerce Cart Abandonment Email

## Context

- **Product**: Direct-to-consumer e-commerce (Allbirds-style)
- **Page/Screen**: Cart abandonment email — recovery CTA
- **Target Audience**: Consumers who added items to cart but didn't complete purchase
- **Agent(s) Used**: CTA Optimization Specialist

## Before (Original Content)

> Subject: "You left items in your cart"
> CTA Button: "Return to Cart"

## After (Agency Output)

> **CTA Variations:**
> 1. **"Complete my order"** [RECOMMENDED] — *Commitment consistency*: Frames the purchase as something already started, not a new decision
> 2. **"Grab them before they're gone"** — *Scarcity/loss aversion*: Implies items may sell out
> 3. **"Still thinking? Here's 10% off"** — *Incentive + empathy*: Acknowledges hesitation, provides a nudge
> 4. **"See what's in my cart"** — *Low-pressure curiosity*: Reduces perceived commitment to just "looking"
> 5. **"Check out now"** — *Direct urgency*: Minimal friction, clear action
>
> **Subject line:** "Your [Product Name] is waiting for you"
> **Post-CTA reassurance:** "Free shipping on orders over $75. Easy returns within 30 days."
>
> **A/B test plan:** Test #1 vs #4. Hypothesis: Low-pressure "See what's in my cart" will outperform "Complete my order" for high-value carts ($100+) where the decision is bigger. For low-value carts (<$50), the direct framing should win.

## What Changed & Why

| Change | Before | After | Principle |
|--------|--------|-------|-----------|
| Passive CTA | "Return to Cart" | "Complete my order" | "Return" is going backward; "Complete" is finishing something started |
| Generic subject | "You left items in your cart" | "Your [Product Name] is waiting" | Personalize with the actual product; "waiting" creates urgency |
| No risk reduction | None | "Free shipping. Easy returns." | Address post-purchase anxiety at decision point |
| No incentive option | None | "10% off" variation | Cart abandoners often need a nudge |
| No segmentation | One-size-fits-all | Different CTAs by cart value | Different motivations at different price points |

## Measurable Difference

| Metric | Before | After |
|--------|--------|-------|
| CTA word count | 3 | 3 (but more compelling) |
| Psychological principles | 0 | 5 (commitment, scarcity, incentive, curiosity, urgency) |
| Personalization | 0 | Product name in subject |
| Risk reduction | 0 messaging | Shipping + returns addressed |
| Segmentation | None | Cart value-based strategy |
